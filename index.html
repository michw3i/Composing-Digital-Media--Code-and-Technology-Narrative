My Code and Digital Technology Narrative

Introduction

I'm a sophomore majoring in Computer Science, and I'm from outside of Philly (in the King of Prussia area). One of my earliest experiences with digital technology was playing Roblox as a child. I spent hours exploring games and customizing my avatar, but I was always curious about how the games worked and how people built these games in the first place. I was first introduced to Python in high school, and I actually found it interesting, which helped me realize that coding was something I could enjoy learning. When I started coding later on in school, it felt very different at first. Writing code was confusing and frustrating, and I didn't get it right away. But every time something finally worked, it reminded me of that same curiosity I had back then on Roblox. That early experience made coding feel less intimidating and more like something I could grow into, and it played a big role in why I decided to major in Computer Science.

Everyday Code

Code shapes how we think, act, and move through the world by influencing our habits and routines. Apps and platforms use algorithms to guide what we click, how long we stay, and how long we return. Completing the Dear Data visualization showed me I spend more time on my phone than I realized, especially of social apps, while my laptop is mostly for schoolwork. The data shows that I spend time on, Instagram, TikTok, and Messages on my phone, but Chrome, Terminal, and PowerPoint dominate my laptop.

Code and digital technology impact on education

Code powers the apps and tools we use every day, making learning more interactive, personalized, and accessible. Algorithms can recommend resources, track progress, and tailor content to our needs. We can collaborate instantly, and explore topics more deeply. Understanding code helps us see how platforms shape our experiences, recognize algorithmic biases, and make informed choices online.
